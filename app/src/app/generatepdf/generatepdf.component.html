Scope:

An External Review of Unimoni Pty Ltd was undertaken to inspect the level of compliance, Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Terrorism Financing Program, Reporting Procedures and Record Keeping Obligations, as a reporting entity registered with (AUSTRAC) Australian Transactions Report and Analysis Center under relevant sections of Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Terrorism Financing Act 2006.

The AUSTRAC CEO may require a reporting entity to appoint an external auditor under subsections 161(2) or 162(2). However, this is an engagement mandated by the Compliance Program Part A, as External Auditors, our duty included inspecting the compliance report, look at its implementation, identify the gaps between the requirement and practice, suggest remedial actions and follow it up for rectification within a reasonable time. If required, a copy of this report should be sent to the CEO of AUSTRAC.

We follow the basis of Compliance Program Review Protocols set by Australian Compliance Institute, we have endeavored to review this entityâ€™s risk approach and adequacy and effectiveness of compliance program based on documents availed to us and chosen samples of random check and therefore cannot assure complete correctness of the probable gap between principles and practices.

AML and CTF review Independence Statement

We confirm that, to the best of our knowledge and belief, we currently meet the independence requirements of the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter Terrorism Financing Act 2006 in relation to the AML and CTF external review of the compliance program, policies and procedures. In certain cases, employees of this entity may have attended public trainings conducted by AML CTF Specialists and or presented by us.

Confidentiality Statement

In order to maintain the integrity and credibility of the risk assessment processes and to protect the parties involved, it is understood that the assessors will not divulge any private and sensitive data to unauthorized persons any information obtained during this risk assessment unless legally obligated to do so.

How to read this report

We have listed expected standard and detail of each component of AML CTF compliance from the legislative and regulatory guidelines perspective, then observed the policies and procedures with the view of adequacy of coverage and efficiency in practice with consideration to enterprise wide risk-based approach. Considered and appreciated the best practices whilst listed out the gaps as recommendations. The grading is purely an effort to provide clarity to stake holders with lesser understanding of the technicalities, read one as poor and 10 being the best practice.
